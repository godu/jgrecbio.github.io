Caesar cipher and modular arithemtic

Introduction

Criptography is the science of keeping a message secret through the usage of encryption. One can date back the usage of ciphers to the dawn of any written civilization. Caesar himlself refered to a certain type of substitution cipher in his book about the conquest of the Gaulle. For this reason, this peculiar encryption scheme was coined a caesar cipher. Although the ceasar's cipher is easy to break, the study of such an encryption method is helpul to illustrate certain mathematical tools: and among them a bit of modular arithmetic and of number theory.
